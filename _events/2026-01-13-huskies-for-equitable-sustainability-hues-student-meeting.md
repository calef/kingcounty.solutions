  class="info-box alignright"> <h3>Next meeting</h3> <p><strong>Tuesday, January 13
  6-7 p.m., HUB 337</strong></p> <p>Learn how you can get involved with sustainability
  projects and make an impact on campus.</p> <p><a class="uw-btn btn-sm" href="https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSdtv9QRigvFZ-6Z8eX8Ki3CbPK6VnM5746Ell0-gnpeo0O2yA/viewform">Sign
  up</a></p> </div> <p><em>Growing together through projects, skills, and community
  - <strong>You are welcome here!</strong></em></p> <p>HuES is open to all UW students
  who care about sustainability, equity, and building community. You don''t need prior
  experience - just curiosity and a willingness to learn and collaborate.</p> <h2>What
  is HuES?</h2> <p>Huskies for Equitable Sustainability (HuES) is an opportunity for
  students to actively engage in the UW''s sustainability efforts, organized by UW
  Sustainability. HuES will connect students with projects that impact sustainability
  on our campus and in our community. You''ll get the chance to work together on tangible
  efforts - whether that’s contributing to UW’s Sustainability Action Plan, volunteering
  at existing sustainability events or projects, or helping develop your own student-led
  initiatives. Whether you volunteer at an event or jump in to a project, you''ll
  leave not just with ideas but with something you''ve helped bring to life.</p> <p>In
  addition, HuES will host workshops to grow your sustainability knowledge and skills.
  You''ll gain experiences that translate into leadership opportunities on campus
  - and beyond.</p> <h2>Who can participate?</h2> <p><strong>All students are welcome
  and encouraged</strong> to join HuES. You can drop in to any meeting to learn more
  and discover how you can get involved.</p> <p>HuES is intended to be more than just
  a club; it’s a community. We’re carving out space not only to work and learn, but
  also to connect with each other as peers and friends. HuES will be led by students,
  allowing for deeper peer-to-peer connections, while UW Sustainability staff will
  be on hand to provide insight on how best to move projects forward. Expect time
  for informal conversations, social events, and shared meals. Because building a
  sustainable future starts with building relationships.</p> <h2>How often will HuES
  meet?</h2> <p>HuES meetings will be held every other week and are open to everyone.
  In between there will be optional working meetings primarily for the student leadership
  board and project leads.</p> <p>Meetings will be held every other week on Tuesdays
  at 6 p.m. <strong>The first meeting of the Winter quarter will be Jan. 13 in HUB
  337.</strong></p> <h2>How to be a part of HuES</h2> <p>HuES is open to all UW students
  who care about sustainability, equity, and building community. You don’t need prior
  experience - just curiosity and a willingness to learn and collaborate. </p> <p>We
  know there are lots of students who are interested in sustainability and want to
  make a difference, but don''t know where to start or how to move their ideas forward.
  HuES helps connect you with existing volunteer opportunities, RSOs and projects
  - or lets you pitch your ideas to students who want to help. </p> <h3>Easy ways
  to get involved:</h3> <ul> <li> <strong>Sign up</strong>: <a href="http://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSdtv9QRigvFZ-6Z8eX8Ki3CbPK6VnM5746Ell0-gnpeo0O2yA/viewform">Fill
  out our interest form</a> so we can keep you updated on meetings, events, and projects.</li>
  <li> <strong>Come to a meeting or event</strong>: See above for the meeting schedule.
  You’re welcome to drop in anytime, whether you want to check it out once or become
  a regular.</li> <li>HuES members can choose to get engaged in a variety of ways
  , such as event planning, campus campaigns, volunteering, and hands-on projects
  throughout the year. All of these opportunities are a great way to grow your knowledge,
  build skills, meet people, and make a tangible impact.</li> </ul> <h3>What You’ll
  Get Out of HuES:</h3> <ul> <li>Community of students who care about change</li>
  <li>Workshops and trainings to grow your skills</li> <li>Leadership opportunities
  to run events and projects</li> <li>A chance to help shape sustainability efforts
  at UW</li> </ul> <p><a href="https://www.instagram.com/sustainableuw/">Follow UW
  Sustainability on Instagram</a> to learn about events, meetings and other HuES opportunities.</p>
  <p>If you have any questions, please reach out to Emily Adams at <a href="mailto:emiadams@uw.edu?subject=HuES">emiadams@uw.edu</a>.</p>

Join the Huskies for Equitable Sustainability (HuES) student meeting on January 13, 2026, from 6:00 PM to 7:00 PM at the Student Union Building (HUB 337). This event invites all University of Washington students interested in sustainability and equity to engage in impactful projects and community building. Attendees can expect to learn about various sustainability initiatives, volunteer opportunities, and how to contribute to UW’s Sustainability Action Plan. No prior experience is necessary—just a willingness to collaborate and explore new ideas. The meeting will foster informal conversations and connections among peers, making it a great opportunity to meet like-minded individuals. HuES meets bi-weekly, so this is a perfect chance to get involved and help shape sustainability efforts on campus. Come discover how you can make a difference while gaining valuable skills and experiences!
