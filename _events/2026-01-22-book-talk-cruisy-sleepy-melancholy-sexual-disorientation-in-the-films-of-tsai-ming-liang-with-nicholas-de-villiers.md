Join us for a thought-provoking book talk titled "Cruisy, Sleepy, Melancholy: Sexual Disorientation in the Films of Tsai Ming-liang" with Nicholas de Villiers on Jan. 22, 2026, at 3:30 p.m. Held in Thomson Hall, this event invites attendees to explore the intricate themes of sexual disorientation in Tsai Ming-liang's films. Expect an engaging discussion that delves into the emotional landscapes and cinematic techniques that define the filmmaker's work. Whether you're a film enthusiast, a scholar, or simply curious about contemporary cinema, this talk promises to enrich your understanding of Tsai's unique storytelling style. Don't miss this opportunity to engage with the ideas presented by Nicholas de Villiers in a vibrant community setting.
