  <p>\U0001F389\U0001F32E Step right up, food aficionados and culinary adventurers!
  \U0001F32E\U0001F389</p> <p>Are you ready to embark on a tantalizing journey through
  the rich tapestry of Mexican cuisine? Look no further than our exclusive Tamale
  Cooking Class with the one and only Carmen Cejudo at El Centro de la Raza!</p> <p>Join
  us for an unforgettable experience where you’ll learn the secrets behind crafting
  the perfect tamale from scratch. Under Carmen’s expert guidance, you’ll unlock the
  mysteries of masa, master the art of filling, and wrap up your creations in traditional
  corn husks, all while immersing yourself in the vibrant culture that surrounds this
  beloved dish.</p> <p>But before you dive into this culinary adventure, let’s take
  a moment to savor the history of tamales. Dating back thousands of years to the
  ancient civilizations of Mesoamerica, tamales have been a cornerstone of Mexican
  cuisine, revered for their versatility, flavor, and cultural significance. Originally
  prepared as portable sustenance for warriors and travelers, tamales evolved over
  time into a cherished culinary tradition enjoyed during celebrations, holidays,
  and everyday meals.</p> <p>Today, tamales continue to captivate taste buds around
  the world, transcending borders and bringing people together with each delectable
  bite. And now, you have the opportunity to become a part of this timeless tradition
  by joining us at El Centro de la Raza for our Tamale Cooking Class with Carmen Cejudo.
  Taking place from 12:30 pm to 2:30 pm.</p> <p>We will be focusing on vegetarian/vegan
  tamal options for this class, with the following flavors:</p> <p>Cheese &amp; Jalapeno
  (Vegetarian) Potato Mole (Vegan) Spinach &amp; Mushroom (Vegan)</p> <p>Supplies,
  food ingredients, and recipes for you to take home are all included with admission.</p>

Join us for the La Cocina Cooking Class on Dec. 13 from 12:30 to 2:30 p.m. at El Centro de la Raza, located at 2524 16th Ave. S, Seattle, WA. This engaging class, led by the talented Carmen Cejudo, will immerse you in the art of making tamales from scratch. Participants will learn to prepare vegetarian and vegan options, including cheese and jalapeño, potato mole, and spinach and mushroom, while also exploring the rich history and cultural significance of this beloved Mexican dish. All supplies, ingredients, and recipes to take home are included in the $100 admission fee. Don’t miss this opportunity to enhance your culinary skills and connect with the vibrant traditions of Mexican cuisine!
