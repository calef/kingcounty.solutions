  class="tribe-events-single-event-description tribe-events-content"> <p><strong>Join
  us for a KC CONNECT Online Workshop!</strong></p> <p>These free, youth-centered
  sessions are open to young people and their families across King County. Each month,
  we explore a different real-life topic related to conflict, communication, and justice
  all in a welcoming virtual space.</p> <p><strong>Workshops take place on the 2nd
  Saturday of each month from 11am to 1pm online.</strong></p> <p>No prep needed-
  just bring yourself, your voice, and an open mind!</p> <p><strong>Topics are grouped
  into three modules:</strong></p> <p><strong>Module 1: Foundations of Conflict Resolution</strong></p>
  <ul> <li> <u>October 11th, 2025:</u> Intro to Conflict – Understanding the Roots</li>
  <li> <u>November 8th, 2025:</u> Communication that Builds Trust</li> <li> <u>December
  13th, 2025:</u> Emotion in Conflict</li> <li> <u>January 10th, 2026:</u> Power,
  Privilege, and Identity in Conflict</li> </ul> <p><strong>Module 2: Skill Building</strong></p>
  <ul> <li> <u>February 14th, 2026:</u> Facing the Conflict</li> <li> <u>March 14th,
  2026:</u> Restorative Response to Harm</li> <li> <u>April 14th, 2026:</u> Family
  Repair Circles</li> <li> <u>May 9th, 2026:</u> Digital Conflict &amp; Social Media
  Drama</li> </ul> <p><strong>Module 3: Critical Awareness and Cultural Power</strong></p>
  <ul> <li> <u>June 13th, 2026:</u> Conflict as a Tool for Change</li> <li> <u>July
  18th, 2026:</u> Youth Circles for Change</li> <li> <u>August 8th, 2026:</u> Taking
  Back Power</li> </ul> <p> </p> <p>To learn more: <a href="https://kcdrc.org/school-youth-mediation/">Youth
  &amp; School Conflicts – Dispute Resolution Center of King County</a></p> <p>To

Join the KC CONNECT: 2nd Saturdays Online Workshop on Jan. 10, 2026, from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m., hosted virtually in Washington. This free, youth-centered workshop welcomes young people and their families from across King County. Each month, attendees will engage in discussions on real-life topics related to conflict, communication, and justice in a supportive online environment. Participants can expect to explore themes such as "Power, Privilege, and Identity in Conflict." No preparation is necessary—just bring your voice and an open mind. This workshop is part of a series designed to empower youth with skills for conflict resolution and personal growth. Don't miss this opportunity to learn and connect!
