A Philadelphia man has been sentenced to 33 months in prison for making violent threats and engaging in cyberstalking, violating civil rights laws. The defendant was found guilty of targeting a woman with a series of harassing messages and threats over social media and through other electronic means. These actions included threats of physical harm, which were intended to intimidate and coerce the victim. This case highlighted the serious nature of cyberstalking and the impact of online harassment on individuals’ safety and well-being. The sentencing underscores the commitment of law enforcement and the justice system to address such crimes and protect victims. The U.S. Attorney’s Office has emphasized the importance of holding perpetrators accountable to deter future acts of violence and harassment. The case serves as a reminder that cyberstalking and threatening behavior will be prosecuted vigorously to uphold civil rights and ensure the safety of individuals in the community.
