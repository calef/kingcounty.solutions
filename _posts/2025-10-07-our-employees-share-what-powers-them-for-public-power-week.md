Seattle City Light celebrates Public Power Week by highlighting the contributions of its employees towards providing reliable and affordable power. Various team members shared their motivations and experiences within the utility. Aditi Duggal, a Business Relationship Manager, expressed pride in leading a complex billing system integration that enhances customer service. Mike Fiola, a Physical Security Program Manager, emphasized the importance of public service and community impact. Stephanie Gibbons, an Electrical Service Representative, was inspired by her husband’s longstanding career at City Light, valuing team camaraderie. Generation Supervisor Brandt March reflected on the legacy of the Skagit Hydroelectric Project, ensuring safe and environmentally conscious power generation. Lineworker Instructor Brandon Melland noted the significance of training future lineworkers for reliable service. Kelvin Moore, a Chief Warehouser, highlighted the warehouse's role in supporting field operations. Edward Park, a Trading Supervisor, shared pride in navigating market volatility effectively. Chris Robertson, an Electrification Projects Coordinator, encouraged those considering a public power career to join a supportive and diverse environment. Jason Timothy reminisced about a storm response that showcased teamwork and dedication. Amy Wheeless, a Strategic Advisor, expressed excitement about implementing clean energy policies. Public power utilities prioritize community needs over profit, ensuring transparency and accountability.
