  ### Federal disaster assistance available to King County residents and businesses affected by November bomb&nbsp;cyclone

  _King County renters, homeowners, nonprofits and businesses affected by the storm may be eligible for low-interest federal disaster&nbsp;loans._

  **News**

  Disaster assistance through the U.S. Small Business Administration, SBA, is available to help King County residents and businesses recover from the November bomb&nbsp;cyclone.

  For more information and to apply online, visit [**SBA.gov/disaster**](https://www.sba.gov/funding-programs/disaster-assistance).

  Applicants can also receive in-person application assistance at an SBA Disaster Loan Outreach Center, starting Monday, Jan. 6. The centers are located at Bellevue Library and King County Elections in Renton. Visit [kcemergency.com](https://kcemergency.com/2025/01/02/storm-damage-you-may-qualify-for-a-low-interest-loan/) for center operating hours.

  Information that may be needed when applying for a loan includes:

  - Name and address of the damaged property.
  - Contact information for all applicants and/or&nbsp;owners.
  - Social Security numbers of all applicants.
  - Insurance policy details, including the agent or carrier contact information and any claims that have been&nbsp;filed.
  - Lease agreement information for&nbsp;renters.

  Applicants can call the SBA Customer Service Center at **800–659–2955** or email **disastercustomerservice@sba.gov**. For people who are deaf, hard of hearing or have a speech disability, please dial **711** to access telecommunications relay services.

  The deadline to apply for property damage is Feb. 21, 2025, and the deadline to apply for economic injury is Sept. 23,&nbsp;2025.

  [Read the SBA’s full announcement here](https://www.sba.gov/article/2024/12/23/sba-offers-disaster-assistance-washington-businesses-residents-affected-bomb-cyclone).

King County residents, including renters, homeowners, nonprofits, and businesses impacted by the November bomb cyclone, may qualify for low-interest federal disaster loans through the U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA). To apply, individuals can visit SBA.gov/disaster for online applications or receive in-person assistance at Disaster Loan Outreach Centers starting January 6, located at Bellevue Library and King County Elections in Renton. Essential information for loan applications includes the damaged property's name and address, contact details for applicants, Social Security numbers, insurance policy details, and lease agreements for renters. For assistance, applicants can contact the SBA Customer Service Center at 800–659–2955 or email disastercustomerservice@sba.gov. Individuals with hearing impairments can use telecommunications relay services by dialing 711. The application deadlines are February 21, 2025, for property damage and September 23, 2025, for economic injury.
