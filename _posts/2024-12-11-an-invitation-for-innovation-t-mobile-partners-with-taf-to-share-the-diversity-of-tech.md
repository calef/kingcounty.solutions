T-Mobile recently hosted students from TAF@Saghalie at its 5G Tech Hub, providing them with a firsthand look at the company's tech innovations. The students toured tech labs, engaged with professionals, and participated in a panel discussion, aimed at showcasing the diverse opportunities within the tech industry. Neeta Moodbidri, a senior director at T-Mobile, emphasized the importance of representation, sharing her goal to inspire students of color by demonstrating the variety of roles available beyond coding and engineering.

The event featured professionals from various fields, who explained complex concepts such as radio frequency testing and the use of x-rays in product modeling. This hands-on experience sparked curiosity among students, prompting them to ask deeper questions about technology. The panel discussion, moderated by Doaa Abouzeid, included insights on resilience and mentorship from T-Mobile leaders, reinforcing the importance of diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) in the tech sector. 

T-Mobile's commitment to fostering relationships with future STEM talent highlights the significance of corporate partnerships in education, aiming to provide meaningful experiences that inspire the next generation of tech innovators and leaders.
