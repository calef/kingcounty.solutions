  members gathered to celebrate the Grand Opening of our new community food hub.  It
  was truly a day to remember as the renewed space filled with excitement, music,
  and the buzz of connection. This is a dream that's been realized after more than
  ten years in the making, and we've finally arrived!  </p><p class=\"\">Our adventure
  maps guided everyone through the different stations throughout our new community
  food hub, starting at the community garden where guests painted wooden stakes and
  could chat with RVFB Executive Director, Gloria Hatcher-Mays, to learn more about
  the garden’s purpose. From there, the next stop on the map was the new Commercial
  Kitchen where people could take a look and speak with our Director of Operations,
  Karey Fey, guests also were able to share their ideas for the space. Next to the
  kitchen our Outreach Coordinator, Zara Teger, greeted guests at our Community Connector
  offices and invited them to play a game, matching resources to our partnered service
  provides. Past this station was the grocery store with an opportunity to learn about
  our food service programs. As everyone exited the grocery store, they found the
  warehouse where the annual bag packing race was held. The final stop on this scavenger
  hunt was the Grocery Rescue van where Eunice Antoine, our Warehouse Coordinator,
  spoke with guests about this program.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p class=\"\">Our <a href=\"https://www.sashasummercousineau.com/\"
  target=\"_blank\">emcee, Sasha Summer Cousineau</a>, joined us for her fourth year
  in a row, welcoming everyone. We shared a delicious array of food including some
  of our staff and community members favorite local restaurants including <a href=\"https://umamikushi.com/\"
  target=\"_blank\">Umami Kushi</a>, <a href=\"https://lilredtakeoutandcatering.com/\"
  target=\"_blank\">Lil Red’s</a>, <a href=\"https://www.salimaspecialties.com/\"
  target=\"_blank\">Salima Specialties</a>, <a href=\"https://www.facebook.com/CreamyConeCafe/\"
  target=\"_blank\">Creamy Cone</a>, and <a href=\"https://kingdonutsseattle.com/\"
  target=\"_blank\">King Donuts</a>. <a href=\"https://www.judesoldtown.com/\" target=\"_blank\">Jude’s
  Old Town</a> joined us again this year to highlight their new mocktail bar, Rosette's,
  working out of our kitchen walk-up window to serve tasty mocktails. Near the front
  of our building, the <a href=\"https://www.doubledutchdivasllc.com/\" target=\"_blank\">Double
  Dutch Divas</a> hosted a variety of activities, providing everyone a chance to jump
  with joy.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p class=\"\">Our program kicked off with the <a href=\"https://www.makfailiondance.com/\"
  target=\"_blank\">Mak Fai Kung Fu Lion Dance Club</a>. Three vibrant lions and live
  drumming brought good luck and smiles to our community. The <a href=\"https://www.allcityband.org/\"
  target=\"_blank\">Seattle All-City Band</a> followed, filling the space with music
  that energized the celebration. Sasha embodied the energy and joy of finally being
  in community in our new space. Then our Executive Director, Gloria Hatcher-Mays
  spoke about what the new space means to us, “This new building is a symbol of patience,
  persistence, and dedication, and it’s my absolute joy and delight to share this
  new home with you all.”&nbsp; &nbsp;</p><p class=\"\">Seattle Mayor, Bruce Harrell,
  also spoke about food insecurity in Seattle and the need to prioritize food access.&nbsp;
  Following this, the past 2 winners of the annual bag packing competition, Mika Yamamoto,
  former board member, and Geoff Anderson, facility’s lead architect of Schemata Workshop,
  joined Sasha on stage to announce and crown the 2025 winner, Wyatt, with a sash.
  Wyatt is 15 years old and has been volunteering with us since he was little.&nbsp;
  His winning time was just under 8.5 seconds!&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p class=\"\">This year,
  we recognize special members of our community with the Ed Parks Award. Joe McKinstry,
  Lee Stanton, Jennifer Worth, and Mika Yamamoto, affectionately known as “the A Team”
  were instrumental in finding the space where our new facility has taken root, and
  bringing this project to completion. We held space for lots of gratitude throughout
  the program, Gloria gave flowers to Capital Campaign Committee Co-Chairs, Calandra
  Childers and Jennifer Worth who have supported us and guided us through this project.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p
  class=\"\">The <a href=\"https://www.instagram.com/rbhs_cheerleaders/\" target=\"_blank\">Rainier
  Beach High School Cheer Squad</a> performed two routines closed program with a bang!
  Thank you to everyone who joined us for this very special day. This milestone is
  more than a new building; it’s also about the community that made it possible. We’re
  beyond excited to open our doors to the new community food hub where everyone will
  have access to nourishing food and community resources, support, and care.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n

On September 7, 2025, the Rainier Valley community celebrated the grand opening of its new community food hub, marking a culmination of more than 10 years of effort. The event featured various activities, including a guided scavenger hunt through the hub's stations, starting at a community garden where attendees painted wooden stakes and learned from Executive Director Gloria Hatcher-Mays. Guests explored a new commercial kitchen, engaged with the outreach coordinator at the Community Connector offices, and participated in an annual bag-packing race in the warehouse. 

The celebration included performances by the Mak Fai Kung Fu Lion Dance Club and the Seattle All-City Band, as well as food from local restaurants and a mocktail bar. Mayor Bruce Harrell addressed food insecurity in Seattle, emphasizing the importance of food access. The 2025 bag-packing competition crowned 15-year-old Wyatt as the winner. Special awards were presented to community members who played key roles in the project's completion, and the Rainier Beach High School Cheer Squad closed the program with performances. 

The event underscored the new food hub's significance for the community, providing access to nourishing food and vital resources.
