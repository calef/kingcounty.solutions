  <p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Final Call for Nominations for 2026 Military Child of the Year® Awards </strong> <br><em>Seven military children will receive $10,000 and attend a D.C. gala in their honor</em></p>



  <p>SAN ANTONIO,&nbsp;TEXAS&nbsp;–&nbsp;Just one week&nbsp;remains&nbsp;to&nbsp;submit&nbsp;nominations for Operation Homefront’s 18<sup>th</sup>&nbsp;annual Military Child of the Year®&nbsp;Award. This program&nbsp;honors&nbsp;the extraordinary achievements of America’s military children&nbsp;and those&nbsp;interested in&nbsp;nominating someone&nbsp;must&nbsp;<a href="https://operationhomefront.org/military-child-of-the-year/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">complete the nomination process</a>&nbsp;by 11:59 p.m. CST&nbsp;on&nbsp;Monday, December&nbsp;2.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



  <p>Military children&nbsp;exemplify&nbsp;incredible&nbsp;resilience and strength of&nbsp;character,&nbsp;and&nbsp;Operation Homefront’s Military Child of the Year&nbsp;Award&nbsp;program&nbsp;recognizes and celebrates&nbsp;those who thrive&nbsp;regardless of the&nbsp;challenges common&nbsp;to&nbsp;military life.&nbsp;Seven&nbsp;award&nbsp;recipients will&nbsp;be named,&nbsp;each&nbsp;representing&nbsp;a branch of the armed forces —&nbsp;Army, Marine Corps, Navy, Air Force,&nbsp;Space Force,&nbsp;Coast Guard,&nbsp;and&nbsp;National Guard.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



  <p>Award recipients&nbsp;typically&nbsp;display dedication to excellence in schools and honor societies, civic associations and clubs, sports, and volunteerism. These young heroes embody leadership within their families and communities.&nbsp;Last year&#8217;s&nbsp;Military Child of the Year awardees&nbsp;faced a change of duty stations a combined&nbsp;34 times and lived through&nbsp;283 months&nbsp;of deployments. They also logged 3,488 volunteer hours in the 12 months before nominations.&nbsp;</p>



  <p>All seven&nbsp;Military Child of the Year Award recipients&nbsp;will be invited to attend Operation Homefront’s Military Child of the Year Awards Gala on&nbsp;April&nbsp;23,&nbsp;2026,&nbsp;where they&nbsp;will each&nbsp;receive $10,000, a laptop, and&nbsp;other&nbsp;surprises&nbsp;donated&nbsp;by Operation&nbsp;Homefront’s generous partners.&nbsp;Operation Homefront will provide airfare and lodging for each award recipient and two family members to attend the gala and spend a few days in the Washington, D.C. area.&nbsp;</p>



  <p>From family members and friends, to teachers, coaches, counselors, and clergy, anyone can nominate a military child. Visit <a href="https://operationhomefront.org/military-child-of-the-year/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">operationhomefront.org/military-child-of-the-year</a> to learn more and nominate an extraordinary military child in your life. </p>



  <p><strong>About Operation&nbsp;Homefront:</strong>&nbsp;<br>Operation Homefront is a national nonprofit organization whose mission is to build strong, stable, and secure military and veteran families by improving their financial, emotional, and social well-being. Recognized for superior performance by leading independent charity oversight groups, 83 percent of Operation Homefront expenditures go directly to programs that support tens of thousands of military families each year. Operation Homefront provides critical financial&nbsp;assistance, transitional and permanent housing, and family support services to prevent short-term needs from turning into chronic, long-term struggles. Thanks to the generosity of our donors and the support from thousands of volunteers, Operation Homefront proudly serves America&#8217;s military families. For more information, visit OperationHomefront.org.&nbsp;</p>

Just one week remains to submit nominations for the 2026 Military Child of the Year® Award, hosted by Operation Homefront. The deadline for nominations is 11:59 p.m. CST on Monday, December 2. This prestigious award honors seven military children who exemplify resilience and strength in the face of military life challenges. The recipients, representing each branch of the U.S. Armed Forces, will be recognized for their academic excellence, community involvement, and leadership.

Awardees will attend a gala in Washington, D.C. on April 23, 2026, where they will each receive $10,000, a laptop, and additional surprises from Operation Homefront’s partners. In the previous year, the awardees experienced a combined 34 duty station changes and contributed over 3,488 volunteer hours. Nominations can be made by anyone, including family members, friends, teachers, and coaches. Operation Homefront aims to support military families by improving their well-being, providing financial assistance, housing, and other support services.
