  <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>About the Smile Survey</strong> &nbsp;</h4>



  <p>The Smile Survey is a public/private partnership that includes the Washington State Department of Health, the Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction, local health jurisdictions (LHJs), and oral health professionals, and with funding and planning support from Arcora Foundation. Usually conducted every five years, this survey provides a comprehensive overview of the current state of children’s oral health across the state. &nbsp;</p>



  <h4 class="wp-block-heading">Smile Survey Dashboard</h4>



  <p>The Smile Survey Dashboard allows advocates, analysts, and other interested groups to explore the most recent survey data.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



  <p>While the <a href="https://doh.wa.gov/sites/default/files/2025-09/140319-SmileSurveyFactSheet.pdf">fact sheet</a> and <a href="https://arcorafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Smile-Survey-2023-24-Infographic-DOH-Approved.pdf">infographic</a> provide key takeaways across Washington state, the dashboard allows you to explore data at the county level across demographic groups.&nbsp;</p>



  <p>Arcora designed the refreshed Smile Survey Dashboard with three key levels of analysis:&nbsp;</p>



  <ul class="wp-block-list">
  <li><strong>Snapshots </strong>at the state and county levels that provide quick references on key figures and changes since the last survey (2015-16).&nbsp;</li>



  <li><strong>Comparison views</strong> for you to compare findings across demographic groups within a county, or overall results across counties to identify populations with the greatest need for oral health support.&nbsp;</li>



  <li><strong>A self-service table view</strong> for quick access to data points for specific counties and demographic groups.&nbsp;</li>
  </ul>



  <p>In addition, the dashboard has a county profile view designed to print as a one-page fact sheet. &nbsp;</p>

The Washington Smile Survey 2023–2024 is a collaborative effort involving the Washington State Department of Health, the Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction, local health jurisdictions, and oral health professionals, supported by the Arcora Foundation. Conducted every five years, this survey provides an in-depth analysis of children's oral health in Washington. 

The Smile Survey Dashboard offers users the ability to access and analyze the latest survey data. It includes state and county snapshots that highlight significant trends and changes since the last survey in 2015-16. Users can compare findings across different demographic groups within counties or between counties to identify populations needing oral health assistance. Additionally, the dashboard features a self-service table view for quick access to specific data points and a county profile view designed for easy printing. Feedback from users is encouraged through a dedicated feedback form, enhancing the survey's relevance and effectiveness in addressing children's oral health needs across the state.
