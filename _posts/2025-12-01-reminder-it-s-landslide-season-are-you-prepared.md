Landslide season is here, and residents in prone areas are urged to prepare for potential hazards. The Seattle Office of Emergency Management highlights the importance of understanding landslide risks, especially during heavy rain or rapid snowmelt. Homeowners should assess their properties for signs of instability, such as cracks in the soil or leaning trees. 

The city recommends creating an emergency plan that includes evacuation routes and communication strategies. Residents are advised to stay informed about weather conditions and to report any signs of landslides to local authorities. The article emphasizes that while some landslides are small, they can still pose significant risks to safety and property. 

To enhance community safety, individuals are encouraged to participate in local emergency preparedness activities and to consider taking courses on disaster response. By being proactive and informed, residents can reduce the impact of landslides and protect their families and homes during this vulnerable season.
