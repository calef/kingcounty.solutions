  <p>In late July, Ballard Boys &amp; Girls Club field was full of excitement as youth, staff, and special guests gathered for an unforgettable afternoon of reading, fun, and inspiration. Hosted in partnership with Amazon, the NFL’s Inspire Change initiative, Boys &amp; Girls Clubs of America, and the Smithsonian, TNF For Good brought literacy to life for dozens of Club youth in King County.</p>

  <p>&nbsp;</p>
  <p>The event provided hundreds of books to youth at our Ballard Club, and more than 1,000 to Club youth across King County.</p>
  <p>From the moment kids walked onto the field, they were greeted with energy, joy, and a world of possibility. Each child selected Smithsonian-curated books to take home, ensuring they can continue exploring new worlds and ideas long after the event ended. With Seahawks legend Ben Obomanu, dancers, and mascot Blitz on hand, the youth engaged in hands-on literacy activities and storytelling, making learning fun.</p>
  <p>These carefully chosen stories reflected a wide range of genres and cultural perspectives, allowing youth to see new places, understand different lives, and imagine great futures for themselves.<b></b></p>
  <p>Many families rely on Clubs during summer to keep their kids safe, engaged, and growing. That includes making sure their minds stay sharp and their creativity keeps flowing. Thanks to TNF For Good, Amazon, the Seahawks, and the Smithsonian, our youth didn’t just receive books; they received affirmation that their stories matter and their futures are worth investing in.</p>
  <p>“Having a partner like the Smithsonian curate a collection of books rich in diverse stories and perspectives reflects the vibrancy of our 32 Clubs. At Boys &amp; Girls Clubs, we use these stories to spark a love of reading that fuels imagination, and when a young person’s imagination is ignited, it opens the door to a thriving, full life,” Boys &amp; Girls Clubs of King County CEO Lori Bae said.</p>
  <p>Events like these reinforce what we already know to be true: when young people are given tools, trust, and the right environment, they rise.</p>
  <p>We’re proud to be part of TNF For Good and grateful for partners who invest in our youths&#8217; brilliance. Whether it’s a book that sparks a new dream, a conversation with a role model that opens new doors, or a moment of belonging in a supportive space, it all adds up. And it all matters.</p>

In late July, the Ballard Boys & Girls Club hosted an exciting literacy event, TNF For Good, in collaboration with Amazon, the NFL’s Inspire Change initiative, and the Smithsonian. This initiative brought joy and inspiration to the youth in King County, offering hundreds of books at the Ballard Club and over 1,000 to Club youth across the region. Children were welcomed with enthusiasm and had the opportunity to select Smithsonian-curated books to take home, fostering their love for reading. With the presence of Seahawks legend Ben Obomanu, dancers, and mascot Blitz, youth participated in engaging literacy activities and storytelling sessions, enriching their learning experiences. The diverse selection of stories allowed participants to explore various genres and cultural perspectives, nurturing their imaginations and aspirations. Many families depend on Clubs during the summer for safe and stimulating environments for their children. Lori Bae, CEO of Boys & Girls Clubs of King County, emphasized the importance of these events in sparking a love for reading and supporting youth in realizing their potential. Overall, TNF For Good provided not just books, but also a message of affirmation regarding the importance of their stories and futures.
