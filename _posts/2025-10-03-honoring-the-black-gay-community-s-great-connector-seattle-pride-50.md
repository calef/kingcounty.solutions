Kenny Joe McMullen was a dedicated advocate for the Black gay community in Seattle, primarily focusing on HIV and AIDS prevention and support. He worked with various social-service nonprofits, including the Northwest Kidney Centers and SouthEast Seattle Senior Center, but his most impactful contributions came through Neighborhood House and the People of Color Against AIDS Network (POCAAN). At these organizations, McMullen played a crucial role in connecting individuals to essential services such as health care, housing, and employment. He was widely recognized as a problem-solver in the community, earning a reputation for his unwavering commitment to helping those in need. McMullen's legacy is marked by his dedication to improving the lives of others, particularly in the context of health and social support for marginalized communities.
