  Bellevue seeks applicants for Planning Commission CIosso<time datetime="2025-10-10T15:25:42-07:00" title="Friday, October 10, 2025 - 15:25" class="datetime">Fri, 10/10/2025 - 15:25</time>

  The City Council is accepting applications to fill a vacancy on the Planning Commission. Interested community members who live in Bellevue are encouraged to [apply online](https://bellevue.granicus.com/boards/forms/803/apply ) by 5 p.m., Friday, Oct. 24.&nbsp;

  The Planning Commission advises the council on updates to the Comprehensive Plan and reviews land use regulations, ordinances and related policy matters. The commission typically meets on the second and fourth Wednesdays at 6:30 p.m., with occasional special meetings as needed.

  Commissioners are appointed by the mayor and confirmed by the council to serve four-year volunteer terms. Service on boards and commissions offers community members a meaningful way to help shape Bellevue’s future.

  **Who can apply?**

  Applicants must live in Bellevue. The city values and encourages participation from community members of all backgrounds and experiences.

  **How to apply**

  - **Online:&nbsp;** Complete the&nbsp;[online application.](https://bellevue.granicus.com/boards/forms/803/apply )
  - **In person:&nbsp;** Paper applications available for pick-up and drop-off at the City Clerk’s Office or Service First at City Hall, 450 110th Ave. NE, or the Bellevue Library, Lake Hills Library or Mini City Hall
  - **By email:&nbsp;** [kroberts@bellevuewa.gov](mailto:kroberts@bellevuewa.gov)
  - **By mail:&nbsp;** City Clerk’s Office, P.O. Box 90012, Bellevue, WA 98009-9012

  For more information about the commission’s role and responsibilities, contact senior planner Kate Nesse ([knesse@bellevuewa.gov](mailto:knesse@bellevuewa.gov) or 425-452-2042).

The City Council of Bellevue is inviting residents to apply for a vacancy on the Planning Commission. Applications are due by 5 p.m. on Friday, Oct. 24. The Planning Commission plays a crucial role in advising the council on updates to the Comprehensive Plan and evaluating land use regulations, ordinances, and related policies. Meetings are held on the second and fourth Wednesdays of each month at 6:30 p.m., with additional special meetings as necessary. Commissioners are appointed by the mayor and confirmed by the council, serving four-year volunteer terms. The city encourages applicants from diverse backgrounds and experiences. Interested individuals can apply online or pick up and drop off paper applications at designated city locations, including the City Clerk’s Office and local libraries. Applications can also be submitted via email or mail. For further details regarding the commission's responsibilities, inquiries can be directed to senior planner Kate Nesse.
