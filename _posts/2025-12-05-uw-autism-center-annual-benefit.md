The University of Washington Autism Center held its annual benefit on Dec. 5, 2025, to raise funds for autism research and support programs. The event featured keynote speakers, including renowned autism advocates and researchers, who shared insights on the latest developments in autism treatment and education. Attendees participated in a silent auction, with proceeds going directly to the center's initiatives aimed at improving the lives of individuals with autism and their families. This year’s benefit emphasized the importance of community involvement and the need for continued funding to enhance resources and services for those affected by autism. The event also highlighted personal stories from families who have benefited from the center's programs, showcasing the positive impact of the center's work. Overall, the benefit gathered supporters from various backgrounds, all united in their commitment to advancing autism awareness and support.
