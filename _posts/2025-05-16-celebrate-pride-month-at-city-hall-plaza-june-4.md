   ![The Honor Guard raises the pride flag at City Hall.](https://bellevuewa.gov/sites/default/files/styles/article_featured_image/public/media/article_featured_image/2025-05/pride-on-the-plaza-flag-raising-24.jpg?h=56d0ca2e&itok=5bXq48SK)

  In honor of Pride Month, the City of Bellevue and Pride Across the Bridge are joining together to host [Pride on the Plaza](https://bellevuewa.gov/city-government/departments/city-managers-office/diversity-advantage-initiative/diversity-advantage-events/pride-plaza), an event celebrating our LGBTQIA+ community that will feature music, food and theatrical performances highlighting the talents of queer artists in the community.

  Community members are encouraged to attend the free event at City Hall on Wednesday, June 4, noon-2 p.m.

  The event will open with a pride flag raising ceremony, accompanied by remarks from city leaders celebrating Bellevue's diversity and the contributions of the local LGBTQIA+ community. Edgar Longoria, executive director of Entre Hermanos, will deliver a keynote address.

  The program will conclude with music by a live DJ and light refreshments. Community members can visit resource tables with information supporting the LGBTQIA+ community.

  “Pride Across the Bridge is proud to work alongside our partner, the City of Bellevue, and its incredible teams to build diverse, connective spaces,” said Axton Burton, executive director of Pride Across the Bridge. “Collaborating on Pride on the Plaza brings joy, visibility and belonging to the heart of the Eastside – supporting the LGBTQIA+ residents, workers and visitors who make Bellevue vibrant year-round!”

  Mayor Lynne Robinson added, “The City of Bellevue stands firm in our commitment to being a welcoming city and supporting our LGBTQIA+ residents, workers and visitors. Our community events provide an opportunity to bring all people together to connect, learn from each other and celebrate our rich diversity.”

  In partnership with the Bellevue Downtown Association, the city is also sponsoring Pride banners that will hang along the Grand Connection route downtown in June. The BDA will host the [Paws and Pride Dog Walk](https://www.bellevuedowntown.com/paws-and-pride-dog-walk) downtownon Saturday, May 31, at Downtown Park.

  The City Council plans to recognize Pride Month with a proclamation at the June 3 council meeting.

  For alternate formats, interpreters or reasonable accommodations, please phone at least 48 hours in advance at 425-452-7881 (voice) or email [diversity@bellevuewa.gov](mailto:diversity@bellevuewa.gov). For complaints regarding accommodations, contact the city’s ADA/Title VI administrator at 425-452-6168 (voice). If you are deaf or hard of hearing, dial 711.

In celebration of Pride Month, the City of Bellevue and Pride Across the Bridge are hosting "Pride on the Plaza" on June 4 from noon to 2 p.m. at City Hall. The free event will feature music, food, and performances showcasing local queer artists. It will begin with a pride flag raising ceremony and remarks from city leaders, including a keynote address by Edgar Longoria, executive director of Entre Hermanos. Attendees can enjoy music from a live DJ and light refreshments, as well as visit resource tables offering information to support the LGBTQIA+ community.

Axton Burton, executive director of Pride Across the Bridge, expressed pride in collaborating with Bellevue to create inclusive spaces for the community. Mayor Lynne Robinson reaffirmed the city’s commitment to inclusivity and diversity. Additional activities include Pride banners along the Grand Connection in downtown Bellevue, and the Bellevue Downtown Association will host the Paws and Pride Dog Walk on May 31. The City Council will recognize Pride Month with a proclamation on June 3. For those needing accommodations or alternate formats, contact the city in advance.
