original_content: "<div class=\"wp-block-group is-vertical is-layout-flex wp-container-core-group-is-layout-8cf370e7 wp-block-group-is-layout-flex\">\n<p><strong>Child &amp; Family Services – ParentChild+ Graduation Celebration</strong></p>\n\n\n\n<p>On August 20, 2025, our ParentChild+ Program&nbsp;proudly celebrated the graduation of 50 children&nbsp;who successfully completed the program. ParentChild+ is a Home Visiting program&nbsp;that empowers parents and supports children in their earliest years, helping families build a strong foundation for school readiness and lifelong learning. Each graduation marks not only the growth of the children, but also the confidence and skills gained by their families in supporting their child’s development.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>This year’s celebration was held at a local park along the water, where families gathered for a joyful summer afternoon. Graduates and their loved ones enjoyed a delicious picnic in the sunshine, creating a vibrant community moment filled with laughter, pride, and togetherness.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>We are deeply grateful to our partners—the Seattle Public Library, King County Library, and Seattle Parks and Rec—for their partnership and support in making this special milestone possible.</p>\n</div>\n\n\n\n<figure proudly celebrated the graduation of 34 families and 37 children from our Parents as Teachers (PAT) program at Steel Lake Park in Federal Way. Over the past two years, these families participated in home visits, group sessions, assessments, and resources—each experience tailored to support their growth and needs.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>The graduation was not only a milestone, but also a joyful community picnic that brought together more than 110 family members and supporters. Families gathered to share food, laughter, and movement, starting the evening with lively Zumba, photographs, and dinner, and concluding with a recognition ceremony where each family received their diploma.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>Through the Parents as Teachers curriculum, parents gained skills, confidence, and resources to support their children’s development and embraced their role as their child’s first teacher. The celebration highlighted the strength of community, as families honored one another’s accomplishments and the shared journey of learning and growth.<br>Congratulations to all the families who original_markdown_body: "**Child & Family Services – ParentChild+ Graduation Celebration**\n\nOn August 20, 2025, our ParentChild+ Program&nbsp;proudly celebrated the graduation of 50 children&nbsp;who successfully completed the program. ParentChild+ is a Home Visiting program&nbsp;that empowers parents and supports children in their earliest years, helping families build a strong foundation for school readiness and lifelong learning. Each graduation marks not only the growth of the children, but also the confidence and skills gained by their families in supporting their child’s development.\n\nThis year’s celebration was held at a local park along the water, where families gathered for a joyful summer afternoon. Graduates and their loved ones enjoyed a delicious picnic in the sunshine, creating a vibrant community moment filled with laughter, pride, and togetherness.\n\nWe are deeply grateful to our partners—the Seattle Public Library, King County Library, and Seattle Parks and Rec—for their partnership and support in making this special milestone possible.\n\n![](https://www.elcentrodelaraza.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/2-566x707.jpg)\n\n![](https://www.elcentrodelaraza.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Parents-as-Teachers-3-566x707.png)\n\n![](https://www.elcentrodelaraza.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Parents-as-Teachers-2-566x707.png)\n\n![](https://www.elcentrodelaraza.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/7-566x707.jpg)\n\n![](https://www.elcentrodelaraza.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/8-566x707.jpg)\n\n![](https://www.elcentrodelaraza.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/10-566x707.jpg)\n\n![](https://www.elcentrodelaraza.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Parents-as-Teachers-1-566x707.jpg)\n\n![](https://i0.wp.com/www.elcentrodelaraza.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Image-64-530x707.jpg?ssl=1)\n\n![](https://i0.wp.com/www.elcentrodelaraza.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Image-63-530x707.jpg?ssl=1)\n\n![](https://i0.wp.com/www.elcentrodelaraza.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Image-62-530x707.jpg?ssl=1)\n\n**_Building Health & Access Together in Federal Way_**\n\nThe recent **King County Adult Dental Program (KCADP) event**, held in partnership with El Centro de la Raza, was nearly at capacity with **15 patients scheduled and a growing waitlist**.
