  wp-block-group-is-layout-flex\">\n<p><strong>Child &amp; Family Services – ParentChild+
  Graduation Celebration</strong></p>\n\n\n\n<p>On August 20, 2025, our ParentChild+
  Program&nbsp;proudly celebrated the graduation of 50 children&nbsp;who successfully
  completed the program. ParentChild+ is a Home Visiting program&nbsp;that empowers
  parents and supports children in their earliest years, helping families build a
  strong foundation for school readiness and lifelong learning. Each graduation marks
  not only the growth of the children, but also the confidence and skills gained by
  their families in supporting their child’s development.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>This year’s
  celebration was held at a local park along the water, where families gathered for
  a joyful summer afternoon. Graduates and their loved ones enjoyed a delicious picnic
  in the sunshine, creating a vibrant community moment filled with laughter, pride,
  and togetherness.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>We are deeply grateful to our partners—the Seattle
  Public Library, King County Library, and Seattle Parks and Rec—for their partnership
  and support in making this special milestone possible.</p>\n</div>\n\n\n\n<figure

  <strong>Parents as Teachers Program – Graduation Picnic! </strong></p>\n\n\n\n<p>El
  Centro de la Raza proudly celebrated the graduation of 34 families and 37 children
  from our Parents as Teachers (PAT) program at Steel Lake Park in Federal Way. Over
  the past two years, these families participated in home visits, group sessions,
  assessments, and resources—each experience tailored to support their growth and
  needs.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>The graduation was not only a milestone, but also a joyful
  community picnic that brought together more than 110 family members and supporters.
  Families gathered to share food, laughter, and movement, starting the evening with
  lively Zumba, photographs, and dinner, and concluding with a recognition ceremony
  where each family received their diploma.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>Through the Parents as Teachers
  curriculum, parents gained skills, confidence, and resources to support their children’s
  development and embraced their role as their child’s first teacher. The celebration
  highlighted the strength of community, as families honored one another’s accomplishments
  and the shared journey of learning and growth.<br>Congratulations to all the families
  who completed the program—your dedication continues to inspire and uplift our community.</p>\n\n\n\n<div

On August 20, 2025, El Centro de la Raza celebrated the graduation of 50 children from its ParentChild+ Program, which supports early childhood development and school readiness through home visits. The event took place in a local park, fostering community joy and togetherness. The organization also hosted a successful King County Adult Dental Program event, providing essential dental care to diverse community members and highlighting the importance of language access. KeyBank supported back-to-school efforts by collecting supplies for K-8 students, ensuring they are well-prepared for the school year. Additionally, Comcast Family Summer Movie Nights at Plaza Roberto Maestas offered community bonding through performances and outdoor films. The Bank of America Student Leaders® cohort toured El Centro, engaging with staff and exploring programs that promote leadership and community service. College GANAS students visited Western Washington University, gaining insight into college life to inspire their academic goals. The article emphasizes the importance of community partnership and support in empowering families and youth through various programs and celebrations, showcasing El Centro de la Raza's commitment to building strong, connected communities.
