An Idaho executive, Michael Smith, pleaded guilty to insider trading involving material nonpublic information regarding the impending acquisition of Company-1, where he served as President and Chief Operating Officer. Smith, 48, had access to sensitive information due to his position and was prohibited from trading Company-1 stock under its Insider Trading Policy. On July 26, 2024, he purchased Company-1 shares using a brokerage account belonging to a close associate, Individual-A, based on this insider information. Following the public announcement of the acquisition on August 7, 2024, Company-1's stock price surged nearly 50%. The next day, Smith sold the shares for a profit of approximately $145,754.69, intended to benefit Individual-A. Smith faces a maximum penalty of 20 years in prison for securities fraud, with sentencing to be determined by a federal district court judge.
