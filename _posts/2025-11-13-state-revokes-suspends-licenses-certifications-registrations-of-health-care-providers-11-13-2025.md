The Washington State Department of Health has revoked or suspended the licenses and certifications of several health care providers due to serious misconduct. Key actions include the indefinite suspension of Wendy Amelia Kurinzi and Constance M. Morris, both registered nursing assistants, for abusing vulnerable adults. Jose Antonio Vasquez, a certified nursing assistant, was also suspended for financial exploitation. Jorge A. Brito and Amanda Kay McCoy faced indefinite suspensions for unsafe patient handling and substance use issues, respectively. 

Emily Maria Cook's medical assistant-phlebotomist credential was suspended following abuse allegations. Gladworth Ochoa Flynn, a home care aide, was indefinitely suspended for accepting substantial payments from a patient. Leslie Carol Boettcher was suspended for mentally abusing a vulnerable adult, while Jennifer Lee Crownover's pharmacy technician credential was suspended after testing positive for controlled substances. 

Sasha Veronica Brown faced indefinite suspension for financial exploitation, and Tucker Lawrence Dufrane's pharmacy credentials were suspended for being under the influence while working. Lastly, Lazarus Augustine Wentz was suspended for inappropriate contact with former clients and cannot seek reinstatement for at least five years.
