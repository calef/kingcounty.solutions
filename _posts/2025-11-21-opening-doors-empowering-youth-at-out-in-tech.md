  ## For young people navigating mental health needs, education and career development support can be transformative.

  Vibrant’s&nbsp;[Adolescent Skills Centers](https://www.vibrant.org/what-we-do/community-programs/youth-young-adult-services/adolescent-skills-centers/)&nbsp;offer safe, collaborative environments where people ages 16 through 22 across all boroughs can strengthen essential life skills,&nbsp;continue their education, build confidence, and explore vocational opportunities filled with purpose and achievement.&nbsp;&nbsp;

  This fall, four&nbsp;participants&nbsp;of&nbsp;Vibrant’s&nbsp;[Adolescent Skills Center (ASC)](https://www.vibrant.org/what-we-do/community-programs/youth-young-adult-services/adolescent-skills-centers/)&nbsp;took a step into the future at the&nbsp;[Out-in-Tech](https://outintech.com/)&nbsp;Leadership Institute.&nbsp;Vibrant staff organized the outing and attended on-site to&nbsp;provide support.&nbsp;Celebrating&nbsp;and elevating&nbsp;queer,&nbsp;transgender, and BIPOC&nbsp;voices in&nbsp;technology,&nbsp;the&nbsp;opportunity created meaningful space for young people to explore potential careers, connect with peers and mentors, and see themselves represented in leadership roles.&nbsp;&nbsp;

  Saoirse, 21, has been a part of the ASC for four years, working toward their GED and other schooling. They saw the Institute as an opportunity to&nbsp;represent&nbsp;Vibrant,&nbsp;build professional relationships,&nbsp;and&nbsp;explore&nbsp;what’s&nbsp;next for them.&nbsp;&nbsp;

  “The best part was seeing the campus&nbsp;I’ll&nbsp;hopefully be attending next year,” they said. I also enjoyed meeting new&nbsp;people.&nbsp;Who&nbsp;knew all the D&D nerds would be at the queer tech conference?!”&nbsp;&nbsp;

  Saad, 24, a computer science&nbsp;senior at Queens College, has been with the ASC for six years. For him, the experience was both inspirational and practical.&nbsp;&nbsp;

  “The best part for me was the engaging classes and the informative panel.&nbsp;It made me think a lot about myself&nbsp;and I loved listening to other&nbsp;students’&nbsp;experiences,”&nbsp;he said. “It didn’t feel like work.” He also met professionals he plans to contact about internships, building a foundation for his next steps after graduation in 2026.&nbsp;&nbsp;

  By&nbsp;participating&nbsp;in Out-in-Tech, students experienced firsthand how innovation and inclusion intersect.&nbsp;The event not only introduced them to&nbsp;new&nbsp;opportunities but&nbsp;also encouraged them to see differences as powerful assets&nbsp;and&nbsp;not&nbsp;barriers.&nbsp;&nbsp;

  As Saad put it:&nbsp;“We&nbsp;definitely need&nbsp;more opportunities like this!”&nbsp;

  With&nbsp;opportunities like this,&nbsp;Vibrant continues&nbsp;to open doors helping young people access the spaces, skills, and confidence they need to thrive as their fullest selves.&nbsp;&nbsp;

Out in Tech is dedicated to empowering LGBTQ+ youth through technology and community engagement. The organization offers mentorship programs, workshops, and networking opportunities to help young individuals develop their skills and confidence in the tech industry. By fostering a supportive environment, Out in Tech aims to bridge the gap between marginalized youth and the tech world, providing them with the resources they need to succeed.

The initiative emphasizes the importance of representation and inclusion, encouraging participants to explore various career paths in technology. Through hands-on experiences, young people gain valuable insights and connections that can lead to internships and job placements. The organization also advocates for diversity within tech companies, pushing for policies that support LGBTQ+ employees.

Out in Tech's commitment to community extends beyond skill-building; it fosters a sense of belonging among participants, helping them navigate the challenges they face in both personal and professional spheres. By opening doors and creating opportunities, Out in Tech is shaping the future of the tech industry while empowering the next generation of LGBTQ+ leaders.
