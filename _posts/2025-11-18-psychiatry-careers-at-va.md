  ## Bringing hope, healing and health to Veterans

  The Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) is dedicated to supporting the mental health of Veterans at every stage, from transitioning out of the military to adjusting to civilian life. Each and every day, VA psychiatrists work as part of interdisciplinary teams that provide compassionate, evidence-based mental health care tailored to each Veteran’s unique needs.&nbsp;

  Let’s take a closer look at how VA psychiatrists serve our nation’s heroes and how you can join our team.

  ## Comprehensive, team-based care&nbsp;

  VA psychiatrists collaborate closely with psychologists, social workers, nurses and other health professionals to address their patients’ [whole health](https://www.va.gov/wholehealth/index.asp). Together, they manage a range of mental health conditions, including posttraumatic stress disorder (PTSD), depression, anxiety, substance use disorders and others. This team approach ensures Veterans receive coordinated, high-quality care—no matter where they live.&nbsp;&nbsp;

  ## Evidence-based treatments&nbsp;

  Evidence-based treatments are scientifically tested courses of treatment that have proven effective&nbsp;for a particular mental health condition. VA psychiatrists tailor [evidence-based treatments](https://www.mentalhealth.va.gov/get-help/treatment/ebt.asp) to each Veteran’s needs, priorities, values, preferences and goals. It’s a top priority at VA to provide the best treatment, while showing respect, care and consideration for each individual Veteran.&nbsp;

  ## Opportunities for growth and innovation&nbsp;

  With one of the largest integrated mental health care programs in the nation, VA offers psychiatrists the chance to work across a variety of clinical settings, including outpatient clinics, inpatient units, [telehealth services](https://telehealth.va.gov/type/home) and specialized programs for trauma and addiction recovery. VA also supports continuing education, research opportunities and leadership development—empowering psychiatrists to grow professionally while advancing the field of Veteran mental health care.&nbsp;

The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) is actively seeking psychiatrists to join its workforce in various roles that cater to the mental health needs of veterans. The VA emphasizes the importance of mental health care, providing comprehensive services, including treatment for PTSD, depression, and anxiety. Opportunities exist across the country in outpatient clinics, inpatient units, and telehealth services, with the aim of improving access to care for veterans.

Psychiatrists at the VA benefit from a collaborative environment, working alongside a diverse team of healthcare professionals. The organization offers competitive salaries, robust benefits, and opportunities for professional development, including research and teaching roles. The VA's commitment to mental health care is highlighted by its focus on innovative treatment approaches and the integration of technology in service delivery, such as telehealth options.

Recruitment efforts are particularly aimed at addressing the growing demand for mental health services among veterans, ensuring that they receive the support they need. The VA’s initiatives reflect a broader commitment to enhancing the quality of care and improving the overall health outcomes for those who have served in the military.
