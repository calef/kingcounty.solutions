  <p><b><span data-contrast="none">KIDS CHAPTER BOOKS</span></b><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>
  <p><a href="https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4062250"><span data-contrast="none">Benny on the Case</span></a><span data-contrast="none"> by Wesley King is a </span><span data-contrast="auto">heartfelt character-driven mystery featuring a neurodivergent male protagonist that explores friendship, belonging, and family. [-School Library Journal]</span><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>
  <p><span data-contrast="auto">In Grace Lin’s </span><a href="https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4042693"><span data-contrast="none">The Gate, the Girl and the Dragon,</span></a><span data-contrast="auto"> a Stone Lion cub travels from the spirit realm into ours in order to save his world from a sinister force. Captivating, thrilling, and emotionally satisfying. [-Kirkus]</span><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>
  <p><span data-contrast="auto">Clare is </span><a href="https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4027842"><span data-contrast="none">The Undead Fox of Deadwood Forest,</span></a><span data-contrast="auto"> a lonely silver-tailed fox, who ushers animals’ souls into the Afterlife. When a mysterious badger spirit appears, Clare’s world is turned upside down. Aubrey Hartman’s tale is heartbreaking, marvelously funny, and generously redemptive. [-Kirkus]  </span><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>
  <p><span data-contrast="auto">William Samuels and his father leave their lives as sharecroppers in Texas to join the 1889 Oklahoma Land Rush. </span><a href="https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4015588"><span data-contrast="none">Will’s Race for Home,</span></a><span data-contrast="auto"> by Jewell Parker Rhodes, is an edge-of-your-seat adventure survival story, as Will and his father face the dangers of violence and racism at every turn. [-Lauren]</span><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>

  <p><b><span data-contrast="none">KIDS GRAPHIC NOVELS</span></b><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>
  <p><span data-contrast="auto">“I generally don’t like to recommend sequels, but </span><a href="https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4043310"><span data-contrast="none">Beetle &amp; the Chimera Carnival</span></a><span data-contrast="auto"> by Aliza Layne is an exception. The first book in the series was so delightful and heartfelt, I waited eagerly for the sequel. The wait was well worth it. Just as heartfelt as the first, Layne really captures how scary and monstrous it feels to be a tween girl. And not just in the fact that Beetle is a literal goblin.” [-Eliza E]</span><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>
  <p><span data-contrast="auto">If there’s one thing that can bridge the human/undead divide, it’s donuts. </span><a href="https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4071348"><span data-contrast="none">Dracula’s Brunch Club</span></a><span data-contrast="auto"> by Brian Gonsar is a friendly, funny vampire story with a little bit of bite. [-Kirkus]</span><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>
  <p><span data-contrast="auto">In Gus Gordon’s </span><a href="https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4065178"><span data-contrast="none">Into the Bewilderness,</span></a><span data-contrast="auto"> best friends Luis (the large furry one) and Pablo (the small furry one) decide to venture from their cozy home into the Big City.  It doesn’t go at all well for the two rustic cabin mates – but luckily, they have each other. [-Kirkus]</span><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>
  <p><span data-contrast="auto">In </span><a href="https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4043811"><span data-contrast="none">A Song for You &amp; I</span></a><span data-contrast="auto"> by Kay O’Neill, two young people travel their own paths to personal authenticity. A sweet and thoughtful rumination on the joy of being one’s true self. [-Kirkus]</span><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>

  <p><b><span data-contrast="none">KIDS NONFICTION</span></b><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>
  <p><span data-contrast="none">Through Squamish language and cultural traditions, learn about Indigenous plant relationships and how we are all connected to nature in </span><a href="https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4069659"><span data-contrast="none">The Land Knows Me: A Nature Walk Exploring Indigenous Wisdom</span></a><span data-contrast="none"> by Leigh Joseph and illustrated by Natalie Schnitter. [-publisher]</span><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>
  <p><span data-contrast="auto">In </span><a href="https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4035496"><span data-contrast="none">My Presentation Today Is About the Anaconda</span></a><span data-contrast="auto"> by Bibi Dumon Tok, translated by Nancy Forest-Flier and illustrated by Annemarie van Haeringen, hilarity ensues when animals give class presentations about other animals.  A brilliant use of anthropomorphic animals, serving up facts and entertainment.  [-Kirkus]</span><span data-contrast="none"> </span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>
  <p><span data-contrast="none">“Much like she does in her adult nonfiction book (Marsha), in </span><a href="https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4052894"><span data-contrast="none">One Day in June: A Story Inspired by the Life and Activism of Marsha P. Johnson</span></a><span data-contrast="none"> (illustrated by Charlot Kristensen), author Tourmaline tells Johnson’s story for younger audiences and contextualizes her legacy amidst today’s movements for Black Lives and trans liberation. I needed tissues for this one!” [-Nico]</span><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>
  <p><span data-contrast="none">Author Patricia Morris </span><span data-contrast="auto">Buckley (Kahnawá:ke Mohawk) and illustrator E.B. Lewis (Lenni-Lenape) pay tribute to the courage and perseverance of Iroquois steelworkers in </span><a href="https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4025403"><span data-contrast="none">To Walk the Sky: How Iroquois Steelworkers Helped Build Towering Cities</span></a><span data-contrast="auto">. [-Kirkus]</span><span data-ccp-props="{}"> </span></p>

  **KIDS CHAPTER BOOKS** &nbsp;

  [Benny on the Case](https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4062250) by Wesley King is a heartfelt character-driven mystery featuring a neurodivergent male protagonist that explores friendship, belonging, and family. [-School Library Journal]&nbsp;

  In Grace Lin’s [The Gate, the Girl and the Dragon,](https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4042693) a Stone Lion cub travels from the spirit realm into ours in order to save his world from a sinister force. Captivating, thrilling, and emotionally satisfying. [-Kirkus]&nbsp;

  Clare is [The Undead Fox of Deadwood Forest,](https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4027842) a lonely silver-tailed fox, who ushers animals’ souls into the Afterlife. When a mysterious badger spirit appears, Clare’s world is turned upside down. Aubrey Hartman’s tale is heartbreaking, marvelously funny, and generously redemptive. [-Kirkus]&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

  William Samuels and his father leave their lives as sharecroppers in Texas to join the 1889 Oklahoma Land Rush. [Will’s Race for Home,](https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4015588) by Jewell Parker Rhodes, is an edge-of-your-seat adventure survival story, as Will and his father face the dangers of violence and racism at every turn. [-Lauren]&nbsp;

  **KIDS GRAPHIC NOVELS** &nbsp;

  “I generally don’t like to recommend sequels, but [Beetle & the Chimera Carnival](https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4043310) by Aliza Layne is an exception. The first book in the series was so delightful and heartfelt, I waited eagerly for the sequel. The wait was well worth it. Just as heartfelt as the first, Layne really captures how scary and monstrous it feels to be a tween girl. And not just in the fact that Beetle is a literal goblin.” [-Eliza E]&nbsp;

  If there’s one thing that can bridge the human/undead divide, it’s donuts. [Dracula’s Brunch Club](https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4071348) by Brian Gonsar is a friendly, funny vampire story with a little bit of bite. [-Kirkus]&nbsp;

  In Gus Gordon’s [Into the Bewilderness,](https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4065178) best friends Luis (the large furry one) and Pablo (the small furry one) decide to venture from their cozy home into the Big City.&nbsp; It doesn’t go at all well for the two rustic cabin mates – but luckily, they have each other. [-Kirkus]&nbsp;

  In [A Song for You & I](https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4043811) by Kay O’Neill, two young people travel their own paths to personal authenticity. A sweet and thoughtful rumination on the joy of being one’s true self. [-Kirkus]&nbsp;

  **KIDS NONFICTION** &nbsp;

  Through Squamish language and cultural traditions, learn about Indigenous plant relationships and how we are all connected to nature in [The Land Knows Me: A Nature Walk Exploring Indigenous Wisdom](https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4069659) by Leigh Joseph and illustrated by Natalie Schnitter. [-publisher]&nbsp;

  In [My Presentation Today Is About the Anaconda](https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4035496) by Bibi Dumon Tok, translated by Nancy Forest-Flier and illustrated by Annemarie van Haeringen, hilarity ensues when animals give class presentations about other animals.&nbsp; A brilliant use of anthropomorphic animals, serving up facts and entertainment.&nbsp; [-Kirkus]&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

  “Much like she does in her adult nonfiction book (Marsha), in [One Day in June: A Story Inspired by the Life and Activism of Marsha P. Johnson](https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4052894) (illustrated by Charlot Kristensen), author Tourmaline tells Johnson’s story for younger audiences and contextualizes her legacy amidst today’s movements for Black Lives and trans liberation. I needed tissues for this one!” [-Nico]&nbsp;

  Author Patricia Morris Buckley (Kahnawá:ke Mohawk) and illustrator E.B. Lewis (Lenni-Lenape) pay tribute to the courage and perseverance of Iroquois steelworkers in [To Walk the Sky: How Iroquois Steelworkers Helped Build Towering Cities](https://seattle.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S30C4025403). [-Kirkus]&nbsp;

The SPL staff has shared their favorite children's books for 2025, highlighting a diverse selection that includes both new releases and beloved classics. Among the top picks are "The Pigeon Will Ride the Roller Coaster!" by Mo Willems, which combines humor with a familiar character, and "The Last Cuentista" by Donna Barba Higuera, a gripping tale that blends science fiction with cultural storytelling. Other notable mentions include "Dog Man: Twenty Thousand Fleas Under the Sea" by Dav Pilkey, appealing to young readers with its engaging illustrations and adventure. "I Am Every Good Thing" by Derrick Barnes is celebrated for its empowering messages, while "Watercress" by Andrea Wang, illustrated by Jason Chin, stands out for its beautiful artwork and emotional depth. The list emphasizes the importance of representation and inclusivity, featuring stories that resonate with various experiences and backgrounds. Staff favorites reflect a commitment to enriching children's literature, encouraging young readers to explore new ideas and perspectives through these captivating stories.
