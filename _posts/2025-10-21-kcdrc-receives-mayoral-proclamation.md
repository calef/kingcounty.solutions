On October 20, 2025, staff members from the Dispute Resolution Center of King County (KCDRC) met with Mayor Bruce Harrell at City Hall, where they received a proclamation recognizing Conflict Resolution Month and KCDRC's contributions to conflict resolution. This month serves to highlight the essential work of individuals and organizations that help communities manage disagreements, promote understanding, and strengthen relationships. KCDRC's team of staff and volunteers exemplifies these values by providing resources, training, and support across Seattle and King County. The public acknowledgment of KCDRC's dedication emphasizes the organization's commitment to equipping communities with the skills and confidence needed for peaceful and constructive conflict resolution. KCDRC remains a vital resource for fostering connections and positive outcomes within communities.
