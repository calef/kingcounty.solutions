A former Michigan pharmacist, Raad Kouza, and his brother, Ramis Kouza, a pharmacy manager, were sentenced for their involvement in a health care and wire fraud scheme that resulted in over $15 million in losses to Medicare, Medicaid, and Blue Cross Blue Shield of Michigan. Raad received an eight-year prison sentence, while Ramis was sentenced to five years. The brothers billed for prescription medications they never dispensed at their pharmacies from 2010 to 2019, focusing on high-cost drugs such as antipsychotics and inhalers. They concealed inventory shortages from auditors to facilitate this fraud. In November 2024, a federal jury in the Eastern District of Michigan found them guilty of conspiracy to commit health care fraud and wire fraud. They were ordered to pay approximately $15.5 million in restitution and forfeiture.
