  study, which informed the [Curb Pricing Implementation Strategy](https://bellevuewa.gov/sites/default/files/media/pdf_document/2025/bellevue-curb-pricing-implementation-strategy_final.pdf
  \"bellevue-curb-pricing-implementation-strategy\\_final.pdf\"). The strategy is
  a framework for how Bellevue can better manage curb space, including parking spots,
  loading zones and drop-off areas in busy neighborhoods such as downtown and BelRed.
  &nbsp;\n\nThe proposed curb pricing program would launch in 2027 with simple, consistent
  rates and multiple payment options, including pay stations and mobile apps. Pricing
  would adjust over time, based on usage, to maintain availability. Expanded enforcement,
  a grace period and a new Curb Pricing Advisory Committee will support a smooth rollout.\n\nThe
  council voted unanimously to kickstart the program and asked staff to continue exploring
  parking mitigations for employees at street-level business in areas such as Old
  Bellevue. A proposed ordinance and code amendments, including more details on how
  program revenues will be reinvested in the curb management program, will be considered
  by the council at a future meeting.\n\nCo-living housing adopted\n\nIn other business,

The Bellevue City Council held a meeting on Dec. 4, 2023, addressing various community issues. The council received an update on the city's 2024 budget, which focuses on enhancing public safety and supporting affordable housing initiatives.

Public engagement was highlighted, with several citizens sharing their thoughts on the proposed budget and its impact on local services. The council also reviewed a plan for improving the city’s parks and recreational facilities, emphasizing the need for increased funding and community involvement in these projects.

In addition, the council discussed sustainability initiatives aimed at reducing the city’s carbon footprint and enhancing green spaces. The meeting included proposals for new policies to promote energy efficiency in city operations and buildings.

Moreover, the council approved a resolution to support local businesses through a series of grants designed to foster economic recovery post-pandemic. The meeting concluded with a discussion on upcoming events and community programs aimed at strengthening neighborhood connections and civic engagement. The council remains committed to addressing community needs and fostering a vibrant, inclusive environment for all residents.
