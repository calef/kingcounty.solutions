  The City of Bellevue is accepting applications for funding starting Monday, Sept. 15, for the [Cross-Cultural Center Without Walls program](https://bellevuewa.gov/city-government/departments/city-managers-office/diversity-advantage-initiative/cross-cultural-center-without-walls "Cross-Cultural Center Without Walls").

  This is the second year the program will provide funding to organizations to host free events and activities that bring Bellevue’s communities together across different cultures, encouraging active intercultural engagement, learning and exchange. The program’s goal is to support interactions between people and groups from different racial, ethnic, cultural and other backgrounds.

  The city is hosting a virtual information session on Thursday, Sept. 18, 6–7 p.m., where applicants can learn about the program and the process to apply. [Preregistration](https://events.gcc.teams.microsoft.com/event/6ced3cf9-7d26-4333-9f58-9a211324a5ef@222d2edd-8255-45bd-8597-52141b82f713) &nbsp;is required.

  The first year of programming concluded in May. During that time, 19 organizations hosted 60 cross-cultural events and programs in Bellevue, serving more than 4,000 people. The city collected feedback from program participants, which informed the program details for this year.

  For alternate formats, interpreters or reasonable accommodations, please phone at least 48 hours in advance 425-452-7855 (voice) or email [diversity@bellevuewa.gov](mailto:diversity@bellevuewa.gov). For complaints regarding accommodations, contact the city’s ADA, Title VI and equal opportunity officer at 425-452-6168 (voice).&nbsp;

The City of Bellevue is launching the second year of the Cross-Cultural Center Without Walls program, beginning funding applications on September 15. This initiative aims to support organizations in hosting free events that foster intercultural engagement and learning among Bellevue’s diverse communities. The program encourages interactions among individuals from various racial, ethnic, and cultural backgrounds. A virtual information session is scheduled for September 18, from 6 to 7 p.m., for interested applicants to learn about the application process; preregistration is required. In its inaugural year, the program facilitated 60 events hosted by 19 organizations, reaching over 4,000 participants. Feedback from these events has helped shape this year's program. For inquiries regarding alternate formats or accommodations, the city encourages contacting them at least 48 hours in advance.
