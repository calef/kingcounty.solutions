Seattle's Office of Labor Standards has reached a settlement with Handy Technologies, an online platform, over alleged violations of the city's domestic worker ordinance. The agreement stems from complaints that Handy failed to comply with regulations designed to protect the rights of workers in the home services industry. As part of the settlement, Handy will pay $1.5 million in restitution to affected workers and implement measures to ensure future compliance with labor laws. This action highlights the city's commitment to safeguarding the rights of domestic workers and enforcing labor standards in the gig economy. The settlement is seen as a significant step towards holding companies accountable for their treatment of workers and promoting fair labor practices.
