This week, we published five important updates for King County residents, highlighting significant advancements in community health, youth engagement, neighborhood initiatives, and transit options. These updates reflect ongoing efforts to enhance local services and foster community involvement, making it a crucial week for residents to stay informed.

### Community Health Initiatives
Snoqualmie Valley Health is making strides in healthcare accessibility with the opening of a new clinic in North Bend. The clinic, located in the former Chase Bank Building, features six exam rooms and aims to provide primary care services led by Dr. Shantal Postiglione. This initiative is part of a broader commitment to reduce travel barriers for residents and expand healthcare options in the area. For more details, visit [Snoqualmie Valley Health Expands Access to Care with New North Bend Clinic](https://snoqualmievalleyhealth.org/svh-expands-access-to-care-with-new-north-bend-clinic/).

Additionally, Snoqualmie Valley Hospital has welcomed Dr. Jimmy Young, a specialist in neurology and epilepsy. This new service will offer personalized treatment plans for epilepsy and seizure disorders, allowing patients to receive expert care closer to home. Learn more about this development in the post [Expert Epilepsy and Seizure Care Now Available at Snoqualmie Valley Hospital](https://snoqualmievalleyhealth.org/expert-epilepsy-and-seizure-care-now-available-at-snoqualmie-valley-hospital/).

### Youth Engagement and Advocacy
Henry Ehrnman, a recent graduate of Mount Si High School, has been recognized for his outstanding community service with the Sophie Theriault Empower Youth Voice Scholarship. His extensive volunteer work, including serving as a Student Representative on the Snoqualmie Valley School District Board, underscores the vital role of youth in community decision-making. Ehrnman’s story exemplifies how young people can impact their communities positively. Read more about his journey in [Henry Ehrnman’s Volunteer Story](https://empoweryouthnetwork.org/henry-erhnmans-scholarship-story/).

### Neighborhood Engagement Opportunities
The City of Tukwila is inviting residents to participate in a neighborhood meeting on October 14, 2025. This event will provide an opportunity for community members to learn about city initiatives and share their input on local issues. The meeting will take place at Billy Baroo’s Restaurant from 6:00-8:00 PM, and light refreshments will be served. For more information, check out the announcement [Join us for a Interurban Avenue South and Foster Point neighborhood meeting on October 14, 2025](https://www.tukwilawa.gov/join-us-for-a-interurban-avenue-south-and-foster-point-neighborhood-meeting-on-october-14-2205/).

### Transit Options for Local Events
As local sports events ramp up, Sound Transit is encouraging fans to utilize public transit options to reach venues like Lumen Field and Climate Pledge Arena. With various transit options available, including Link light rail and Sounder trains, residents are reminded to plan ahead and consider sustainable travel methods. For tips on navigating transit during the sports season, read [Take transit to the game!](https://www.soundtransit.org/blog/platform/take-transit-to-game).
