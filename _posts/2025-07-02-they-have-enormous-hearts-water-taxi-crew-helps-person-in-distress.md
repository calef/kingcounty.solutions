On March 16, the crew of the King County Water Taxi Sally Fox responded to a person in distress at Seacrest Dock in West Seattle. Deckhand Zack Klaja noticed someone behaving unusually near the guardrail and alerted Captain GW Rogers. The crew's vigilance increased as they prepared for a potential emergency. When the individual fell into Elliott Bay, the crew immediately acted. Rogers swiftly maneuvered the boat back to the dock while Klaja donned a rescue suit, entering the water to assist the person in distress. Nearby divers joined him, helping to bring the individual to safety until first responders arrived. The crew's regular training enabled a rapid and effective response, with all members displaying professionalism throughout the incident. Captain Rogers praised the teamwork and skills of his crew, emphasizing their readiness and ability to handle such situations seamlessly. This was not Rogers' first rescue; he had previously been recognized for a similar incident in 2021. The crew's dedication to public service and their compassion for those in need were highlighted as exceptional qualities that define their work. Rogers remarked on the crew's enormous hearts and commitment to helping others, making them stand out in their roles.
