  Oct. 22 meeting for businesses, organizations interested in hosting events along 2 Line

  In anticipation of thousands of soccer fans staying at Eastside hotels and other lodgings while the World Cup is in Seattle next summer, the City of Bellevue is inviting businesses and cultural organizations to host celebrations along the 2 Line corridor then.

  Small businesses, local artists, cultural organizations, nonprofits and creative entrepreneurs can learn about grants for World Cup-themed activations at a meeting at City Hall on Wednesday, Oct. 22, 4-6 p.m.

  Activations will take place on six match days in June and July 2026, coinciding with Seattle’s World Cup games. The Bellevue Downtown Association, Visit Bellevue Washington and other organizations will give grants and other support to those hosting activations.

  The city is seeking imaginative, inclusive and globally inspired ideas that celebrate the spirit of the tournament and vibrancy of our region. These activations should enhance not only the transit experience of community members and visitors making their way to the games, but also entertain people not making a trip on the 2 Line.

  At the Oct. 22 session, those interested in hosting events will meet city staff and community partners, learn about the activation timeline, get details on funding and partnership opportunities and share ideas for World Cup celebrations. A soccer-themed mixer will follow the presentation.

The City of Bellevue is preparing for significant celebrations along the 2 Line corridor during the World Cup in Seattle next summer. To facilitate this, a meeting is scheduled for Oct. 22, where businesses, cultural organizations, and local artists can learn about grants available for World Cup-themed events. These activations will occur on six match days in June and July 2026, coinciding with Seattle's games. The Bellevue Downtown Association and Visit Bellevue Washington will provide financial support for these initiatives. The city encourages creative, inclusive, and globally inspired ideas that enhance the experience of both community members and visitors, whether they are heading to the games or not. The Oct. 22 meeting will include discussions with city staff and community partners about the activation timeline and funding opportunities, followed by a soccer-themed mixer for attendees to share ideas for World Cup celebrations.
